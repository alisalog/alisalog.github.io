About

During my time at PPL Corporation, a leading energy company serving millions of customers across the U.S., I was responsible for enhancing and evolving digital experiences for both external and internal customers.

Contributing to the development of a scalable design system in Figma, I established a foundation for consistent and intuitive web and mobile interfaces across PPL’s companies. This included major operating entities such as PPL Electric Utilities, LG&E and KU Energy, and supporting the integration of Rhode Island Energy, ensuring a seamless and cohesive user experience throughout the transition and beyond.

Rhode Island Energy
Website Redesign

Overview

The Rhode Island Energy website redesign was a major digital transformation aimed at creating a centralized, user-friendly experience for residential and business customers. The project aimed to enhance accessibility, simplify navigation, and integrate key self-service features, making it easier for users to manage their energy services efficiently.

The redesign focused on creating an intuitive and visually cohesive platform that streamlined interactions and provided customers with essential tools for account management, energy savings, and outage reporting.

Goal

The existing Rhode Island Energy website presented several usability challenges, including complex navigation, unclear information architecture, and limited self-service capabilities. Users often faced difficulties in accessing key services such as bill payments, outage tracking, and energy-saving programs. The challenge was to simplify the user journey, improve content discoverability, and ensure the platform provided an engaging and efficient experience for all customer segments.

Research & Discovery

The project began with an in-depth analysis of user behavior, leveraging analytics to identify pain points and high drop-off areas. Additionally, qualitative research, including user interviews and surveys, provided insights into customer expectations and frustrations. Key findings indicated that users needed a more intuitive navigation structure, faster access to self-service features, and clearer communication of energy-saving opportunities.

User Analysis & Persona Creation

To ensure the redesign met the needs of diverse customer groups, detailed user personas were developed. These personas reflected the behaviors, goals, and challenges of residential customers, business owners, and energy professionals. By referencing these personas throughout the design process, we were able to craft tailored experiences that addressed specific user needs.

Information Architecture & Workflow Design

The website’s information architecture was restructured to enhance usability and clarity. A key improvement was eliminating the need for users to pre-select between residential and business accounts, simplifying the login experience. Service-related pages were reorganized to ensure users could find relevant information quickly, reducing the number of clicks needed to complete essential tasks.

Prototyping & Usability Testing

Interactive prototypes were developed to test new design concepts with real users. This iterative testing process allowed us to refine user flows, optimize navigation, and validate usability enhancements. Key improvements included:

  • Streamlined account access and bill payment workflows.
  • Enhanced outage reporting and tracking tools with real-time updates.
  • A restructured "Ways to Save" section featuring energy rebates and supplier options.

Visual Design & Interaction

The visual design focused on creating a modern, clean aesthetic that aligned with Rhode Island Energy’s brand identity. A consistent design system was implemented, incorporating accessible color schemes, clear typography, and intuitive iconography. The new interface prioritized readability and ease of interaction, ensuring that customers could navigate the site confidently.

Delivery Process

The success of the Rhode Island Energy website redesign was driven by cross-functional collaboration between design, development, and project management teams.

  • Collaboration with Stakeholders: Regular meetings with stakeholders ensured alignment on business goals and user needs.
  • Developer Integration: Detailed design specifications and component libraries were provided to developers, streamlining implementation.
  • Quality Assurance & Iteration: Post-launch testing and feedback collection allowed for continuous refinement, ensuring an optimal user experience.

Outcomes

The redesigned Rhode Island Energy website delivered significant improvements in usability, customer satisfaction, and digital engagement. Key outcomes included:

  • Increased self-service adoption, reducing call center inquiries.
  • Higher engagement with energy-saving programs.
  • Improved accessibility and compliance with WCAG standards.
  • A seamless, mobile-responsive design ensuring a consistent experience across devices.

Design System Implementation

As a core aspect of the Rhode Island Energy digital transformation, I led the development and implementation of a comprehensive Enterprise Design System in Figma. This initiative provided a unified design language with standardized components, advanced design tokens, and reusable interaction patterns, significantly enhancing visual consistency and scalability.
