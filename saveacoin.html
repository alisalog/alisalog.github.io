PROJECT
OVERVIEW

About

Save-a-Coin is a group project completed for the User Centered Design course at the University of Central Florida. It is a money-saving and tracking app designed to help users take control of their finances with ease and confidence.

Key Features

  • Create a customized profile tailored to your financial goals, location, preferred currency, number format, and more.
  • Link your bank account for easier spending and savings tracking. This feature automatically fetches transaction details, eliminating the need for manual input.
  • Categorize and monitor your expenses in a way that works for you. The simple interface allows for seamless navigation and a clear overview of your financial activities.
  • Create savings categories, set financial goals, and track your progress in real-time by linking your savings account.
  • Discover coupons and deals from your favorite stores for online and in-person shopping. Filter your search with categories.

Timeline

The process for this project began in September 2024. It included multiple steps, each accompanied by a presentation summarizing conducted research, documentation, and completed design. Presentation of the final product took place in December 2024.

PROCESS

Discovery Phase

To lay the foundation for the Save-a-Coin app, we conducted comprehensive research into the most popular budget tracking and saving apps on the market. We analyzed 12 leading apps, including Goodbudget, Ibotta, Monarch, and others. Our goal was to identify the strengths and weaknesses of each app, focusing on usability, >eature set, availability, and reach.

To synthesize our findings, we created a Function Matrix to map out essential features and identify areas where existing apps fell short. This process guided our brainstorming sessions and informed our approach to designing a comprehensive and user-friendly money-saving experience.

Research

The next step in the project was to identify our target audience and stakeholders. After thorough analysis, we determined that the primary users of the Save-a-Coin app are adults, college students, families, and online shoppers, focusing on individuals seeking financial control, expense tracking, and efficient saving strategies. The key stakeholders include investors, business partners, competitor apps, the stock market, developers, and app employees.

To better understand our users, we created three detailed personas to visualize their needs and preferences.

Design Process

The design phase consisted of three stages: Low-Fidelity, Mid-Fidelity, and High-Fidelity prototypes. The Low-Fidelity prototype was a basic sketch of the primary application screens, including the navigation menu, main buttons, and essential sections such as the user account, spending tracker, savings dashboard, and a page for product coupons and deals.

The Mid-Fidelity prototype introduced interactive elements, showcasing the app's core functionality and navigation flow.

Finally, the High-Fidelity prototype represented the complete, polished design with a fully functional user interface, ready for presentation and testing.

DESIGN
SYSTEM

Logo

The design process began with exploring various styles and concepts for the app's logo. My team and I finalized a logo featuring a coin, which became the app’s mascot and a recognizable brand element.

Other Design Elements

Next, we carefully selected a cohesive color palette and a fitting typeface and developed a design system that included custom icons to ensure consistency and visual appeal throughout the app.

OUTCOMES

The final deliverables for the Save-a-Coin project included comprehensive documentation detailing the entire design process, along with the Figma UI design and prototypes at various fidelity levels. We presented our final product in front of other groups and University faculty, showcasing our approach, research, and design outcomes while demonstrating the app’s user-friendly interface and key features.
